The History of NYC's Boroughs

Before New York City became the big city it is today, Native Americans called the Lenape people lived on this land. In the 1600s, European settlers came and started a trading post called New Amsterdam on the southern tip of Manhattan.

In 1664, the English took control and renamed it New York. Over the next 200 years, each borough grew into its own community with its own character and culture.

The biggest moment in NYC history happened on January 1, 1898, when the five boroughs officially became one city called New York.

Important Dates in NYC History

  1. 1626 — Dutch settlers start New Amsterdam
  2. 1664 — English take over and rename it New York
  3. 1883 — The Brooklyn Bridge opens
  4. 1898 — The five boroughs become one city
  5. 1931 — The Empire State Building is finished
